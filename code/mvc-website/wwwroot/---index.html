Redmond Hotel shoot

A very common recipient of drone services is the commercial real estate industry. To complement the interior and ground exterior shots, a shot like this can add a very nice dimensionality and perspective of the building. Hotels and resorts are amongst the most aerially-photographed buildings.

Same data, different information

In many scenarios such as construction sites, mines, landfills, stockyards, etc., there is an interest in knowing elevations of surfaces, including quantifiable data such as volumes of stockpiles. Going beyond inventory of stockpiles, knowing details about the topography and geometric features of the land is of profound interest for surveying and geomatics. Using post-processing with GIS and geospatial tools, we can generate multiple layers depicting different styles and tagged with different information and classification.

Geometrical correctness

This is an example of a typical "orthophoto" or "orthomosaic" photo. On a first observation, it seems to be just a photo taken from an aircraft, manned or unmanned, but there are some key differences: first, they offer a perspective from a higher altitude than allowed for a drone to fly without the proper waivers. Second, a single aerial photograph would introduce distortion due to the angled perspective growing outward from the center of the image, the geometry of the lens, and the topographic features of the area. These distortions are minimized through a process called orthorectifications. Learn more about orthomosaic photos here.

Know your inventory to date

One of the most used deliverables in photogrammetry are volume calculations. Used in material inventory management, cut and fills, and for earth-moving planning. A report can be prepared and delivered to you, or a point cloud can be uploaded to Pix4D Cloud and site managers can calculate volumes online and on-demand with a little ramp-up.

Cellphone tower visual inspection

While drones have pushed the industry forward in terms of efficiency and cost savings, the most valuable contribution is, hands down, the protection of life against hazards. Safety is paramount when lives are involved in performing high risk operations where no matter how well all factors are assessed, there are potential deviations that can result in bodily injury or even the loss of human life. The same way expensive robots are used to inspect hazardous sites, drones are able to perform visual inspections in situations that otherwise are dangerous to observe personally.

3D meshes of worksites

With photogrammetry a lot of possibilities are open for you. From simply "visiting" a site virtually, to inspections, linear, area and volume calculations. By simply going online or loading the model in your laptop or tablet, you can navigate at will throught the details of your site. Zoom in, take interesting measurements, make comments and annotations, share the model with stakeholders, etc. Everything with information as recent as earlier that day.

Make an assesment without moving from your office

Against all our wishes, eventualities happen. And during these you need all the tools you can count on to make the best out of the situation and mitigate all risks. From domestic accidents to full-scale disasters, drones are now a fundamental tool for first responders and insurance companies.

Keep tabs on your progress

The construction industry is a trillion dollar industry. How much of that is on account of delays? It is difficult to understand progress and deviations from just data from traditional progress reports. Obtaining images that are meaningful and provide actionable information is paramount for construction managers. On top of that, you can create 3D models and mapping deliverables that can be compared against earlier reports and blueprints from your BIM solutions. Even though construction is the typical use case, you can keep track of anything aplicable. E.g. Keep track of changes in the erosion of a coastline.

Drone mapping and photogrammetry

Our favorite area of expertise. We fly drones with high quality global shutter cameras, and shoot multiple overlapping images of a point of interest. Then, using a process called "Photogrammetry" we process the images to create 2D maps, and 3D models of the target site. Optionally, we use GPS equipment to geo-locate and improve the accuracy of the model to be certified for more advanced engineering applications. From there we are just limited by imagination.

We can extract a wealth of information and generate multiple deliverables, including but not limited to:

  • Point clouds for 3D modeling.
  • Textured meshes for 3D rendering.
  • Orthomosaic 2D maps.
  • Geolocated and georectified maps.
  • Digital Elevation / Terrain / Surface models.
  • Contour line maps.
  • Hillshade maps.
  • Distance, area and volume estimations.
  • Survey data capture.
  • GNSS ground control shooting.
  • and more...

Aerial video and photography

Before any other application, drones found a niche in the field of photography.

Drones are essentially a high-quality flying camera. They can offer a perspective that no traditional camera can. From high resolution 20 Megapixel HDR photographs, to 4K video and HD upto 60fps and 120fps in slow-motion.

Beyond beauty shots, aerial photography and video (including live video) can be of assistance in many interesting scenarios limited just by your imagination, such as livestock monitoring, news coverage, wildlife observation and it's even becoming a favorite for first responders doing crowd control, disaster assesment, safe fire visual assistance, etc.

  • Commercial and Residential Real Estate photography and video.
  • Event filming and photography, live monitoring and/or streamming to the internet.
  • Live inspection of lands, sites, incidents, etc.
  • Post-Catastrophe and incident documentation and inspection.
  • Assistance in search and rescue (Optical only).
  • Movie footage filming.
  • Marketing and branding footage filming.
  • Camera rig operation.
  • Ground photography.

We are compliant

Drones share the National Airspace with manned aviation. For this reason, the Federal Aviation Administration a.k.a the FAA strictly governs UAV operations through the 14 CFR part 107 set of regulations. We take these rules very seriously and we fully abide by them. Either by observing operational limitations, obtaining waivers or LAANC authorizations, you can rest assured, your operation is in compliance.
